## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 tablespoons Garlic Olive Oil or Clarified Butter
- [ ] 2-3 large Onions, thinly sliced
- [ ] ¼ cup Traditional 18-year-old Balsamic Vinegar
- [ ] 2 tablespoons Brown Sugar*
- [ ] Salt and Pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Peel the onions and slice them thinly, pole to pole, to ensure even cooking. Aim for slices about 2-3 mm thick.
2. In a large enough fry pan over medium heat, heat the olive oil. Once hot (365 °F / 185 °C), add the onions, ensuring they are spread out evenly and sauté for a couple of minutes.
3. Cover and cook, stirring well every 2 to 3 minutes to ensure even cooking and caramelization. This should take about 10 minutes, or until they are a nice dark golden brown.
4. Add the vinegar and sugar (if needed) to achieve the perfect balance between sweet and savoury.
5. If you are using aged (sweet) Balsamic Vinegar, skip the sugar.

## Notes

### What Type of Onions is Best for Caramelizing?

The best onions for caramelizing are typically yellow onions because of their natural sweetness and robust flavor. Red onions can also be used for a slightly different flavor profile, while white onions are less sweet but still work well. The key is to choose an onion based on the desired sweetness and the dish you are pairing it with.

### Do I Need to Add Sugar to Caramelize Onions?

Adding sugar is not necessary to caramelize onions, as onions naturally contain sugar, which caramelizes with heat. However, a pinch of sugar can accelerate the caramelization process and enhance the natural sweetness, especially if you’re in a rush or if the onions aren’t particularly sweet.

### How long Does it Take to Properly Caramelize Onions?

Caramelizing onions is a slow process; it can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour depending on the desired degree of caramelization and how much onions you are cooking. The process should not be rushed as the slow cooking on low heat is what allows the natural sugars to caramelize without burning.

### Can I Caramelize Onions without Oil or Butter?

It is possible to caramelize onions without oil or butter, although fat helps to evenly distribute heat and adds flavor. If you choose to go without, use a non-stick pan and a small amount of water to prevent sticking, being mindful that the onions don’t dry out or burn.

### What is the Best Pan for Caramelizing Onions?

A thick-bottomed skillet or a non-stick pan is ideal for caramelizing onions. These types of pans distribute heat evenly, preventing hot spots that can cause the onions to burn. Cast iron or stainless steel skillets are excellent choices for achieving perfect caramelization.

### Can Caramelized Onions Be Made in Advance?

Yes, caramelized onions can be made in advance. They store well in the refrigerator for up to a week and can be frozen for up to three months. Ensure they are cooled to room temperature before storing and use airtight containers to preserve their flavor and moisture content.

### How Can I Store Caramelized Onions?

Store caramelized onions in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week or freeze them for up to three months. When freezing, spread them out on a baking sheet to freeze individually, then transfer to a freezer bag, which helps in removing only the needed amount later.

### What Are the Common Uses of Caramelized Onions?

Caramelized onions are incredibly versatile and can be used in numerous dishes. They are fantastic on burgers, pizzas, in pasta sauces, on top of steaks, in soups like French onion soup, or even as a part of a savory tart filling. They add a sweet and rich flavor that enhances the overall taste of any dish.

### Can I Use a Slow Cooker to Caramelize Onions?

Yes, onions can be caramelized in a slow cooker. It’s a great “set it and forget it” method, especially when cooking large batches. Simply place sliced onions with a bit of oil or butter in the slow cooker, set it on low for 8-10 hours, and stir occasionally, if possible, to ensure even caramelization.

### What is the Difference between Sautéed Onions and Caramelized Onions?

Sautéed onions are cooked over higher heat for a shorter period, resulting in onions that are soft but not deeply browned. Caramelized onions, on the other hand, are cooked slowly over low heat, allowing the natural sugars to brown and develop a rich, sweet flavor.

### Can I Caramelize Onions in an Oven?

Yes, onions can be caramelized in an oven. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet, toss with oil, and bake at a low temperature (around 375°F) for about 25-30 minutes or until they are soft and have a deep golden color. Stir occasionally to ensure they cook evenly.

### What Kind of Fat is Best for Caramelizing Onions?

Butter and olive oil are popular choices for caramelizing onions, as both add flavor and help in the caramelization process. Butter provides a rich flavor but can burn easily, so it’s often mixed with oil to raise its smoking point. Clarified butter or ghee is a great alternative as it can withstand higher temperatures.

### Why Do My Caramelized Onions Always Burn?

If your caramelized onions are burning, the heat may be too high. Caramelizing onions requires patience and low heat to allow the sugars to caramelize without burning. Also, make sure to stir the onions frequently and add a little water or additional fat if they start to stick too much to the pan.

### How Do I Achieve Perfect Caramelization Every Time?

The key to perfect caramelization is consistency, low heat, and patience. Avoid crowding the pan, as too many onions can lower the temperature and cause them to steam rather than caramelize. Stir regularly and ensure there is enough fat in the pan to cover the onions lightly.

### Are Caramelized Onions Healthy?

Caramelized onions are a healthy addition to dishes when made without excessive fat. Onions themselves are low in calories and high in vitamins and antioxidants. The cooking process can reduce some nutrients but increases the natural sweetness and flavor, making them a great way to enhance dishes without adding sugar.
