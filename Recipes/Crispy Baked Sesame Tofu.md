## Ingredients

### Tofu

- [ ] 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
- [ ] 1 14 ounce (ca. 529 gram) block extra firm tofu
- [ ] 1 tablespoon corn starch
- [ ] 3 tablespoons breadcrumbs I used gluten-free

### Sauce

- [ ] ⅓ cup low sodium soy sauce or tamari
- [ ] 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- [ ] 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- [ ] 2-3 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
- [ ] 1 tablespoon corn starch
- [ ] 4 tablespoons water divided
- [ ] 5 cloves garlic
- [ ] 1 tablespoon olive oil

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 400 Fahrenheit (ca. 204 °C).
2. Drain the excess liquid from the tofu and dry it off with a towel.
3. Cut into 1 inch (2.54 cm) cubes and add to a large bowl. Toss with the soy sauce.
4. Toss with the cornstarch, then the breadcrumbs until all pieces are evenly coated.
5. Add to the baking tray and bake for 30–35 minutes, or until golden brown.
6. During the final 10 minutes, prep the sauce.
7. Mince the garlic and sauté with 1 tablespoon neutral oil in a small sauté pan for 2–3 minutes, or until lightly browned.
8. Add in the soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons of water and sesame oil.
9. Whisk together the cornstarch and another 2 tablespoons of water in a bowl and add to the pan.
10. Heat over low heat for 3–5 minutes, or until the sauce bubbles and thickens.
11. When the tofu is done, toss with the sauce. Serve over rice with steamed broccoli or enjoy as is!

## Notes

- If you have a tofu press or extra time on your hands, definitely press the tofu, but it is not necessary for this recipe. It will make it crispier, but you can easily skip it!
- I always use low sodium soy sauce, if you use regular soy sauce you may want to add a bit of water to the sauce so the saltiness isn’t overpowering.
