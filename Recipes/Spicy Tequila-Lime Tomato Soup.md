## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- [ ] 1 large onion, cut into chunks
- [ ] 4 stalks celery, cut into chunks (_I only used 2_)
- [ ] 5 cloves garlic, chopped
- [ ] 1 green bell pepper, seeded and cut into chunks
- [ ] 2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and chopped
- [ ] ¼ cup tequila
- [ ] 1 (28-ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes (_I used fresh Roma tomatoes_)
- [ ] 2 cups vegetable stock
- [ ] 1 (15-ounce) can of crushed tomatoes (_I used 1/2 small can of tomato paste_)
- [ ] 1 lime, juiced
- [ ] salt and ground black pepper to taste
- [ ] 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro, optional

## Instructions

1. 1. Heat olive oil in a large stockpot over medium heat; cook and stir onion, celery, and garlic until softened, 5 to 10 minutes. Add green bell pepper and jalapeño peppers to the onion mixture; cook and stir until peppers are softened, about 5 minutes.
2. Pour tequila into vegetable mixture and simmer until slightly reduced, 3 to 4 minutes. Add fire-roasted tomatoes, vegetable stock, crushed tomatoes, lime juice, salt, and pepper to vegetable-tequila mixture; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until flavours have blended for about 20 minutes. Stir cilantro into the soup just before serving.
