## Ingredients

- [ ] 400g spaghetti or linguine
- [ ] Two large un-waxed lemons
- [ ] One clove of garlic, peeled and bashed but kept whole
- [ ] 100 ml olive oil
- [ ] One teaspoon of flaky sea salt
- [ ] 50g salted butter
- [ ] 40g Parmesan, grated
- [ ] 15g of basil, leaves picked and torn

## Instructions

1. Put 400g linguine into a large lidded saucepan. Grate in the zest of two large lemons and add one clove of garlic, peeled and bashed but kept whole, 100 ml of olive oil and one teaspoon of flaky sea salt.
2. Add one litre of boiling water, cover with a lid and bring to the boil. As soon as it boils, remove the lid and simmer for eight minutes.
3. Using a pair of tongs to turn the pasta in the thickening pasta water every 30 seconds or so as it cooks.
4. Once the pasta has had eight minutes to simmer, squeeze in the juice of one of the zested lemons and simmer for a final two minutes with the lid off.
5. Once almost all the water has evaporated, take the pan off the heat, stir in 50g of salted butter and 20g of grated Parmesan and leave to sit for a minute or two.
6. Taste and add more salt, lemon juice and butter or olive oil as needed. Tangle into four bowls and finish with the rest of the Parmesan and a bunch of torn basil leaves, if you like.
7. Such a simple pasta can taste delicious, although some people might fancy more to chew on, such as prawns.

## Notes

Seafood can be a great addition to a lemon-based pasta sauce, but even chicken could work well with the flavourings.
