This scallop curry is creamy, fragrant, sweet, savoury and a little bit spicy. With less than 10 ingredients and 30 minutes, this dinner is easy to make any night of the week. Coconut milk and spicy Thai red curry paste pair up to take your taste buds to Thailand. Ready? Let's go! (This is gluten-free and dairy free.)

## Ingredients

- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon Vegetable oil
- [ ] 1/2 cup Onion, finely chopped
- [ ] 1/2 clove Garlic, minced
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon Ginger, finely grated
- [ ] 1 tablespoon Red Curry Paste (Add up to 2 tablespoons more if you want this really spicy) I use Thai Kitchen brand curry paste.
- [ ] 400 ml Coconut Milk (2 cans) I use Thai Kitchen regular coconut milk
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon Lime Juice (or to taste)
- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon Sugar (or to taste)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste) For authentic flavour, use fish sauce instead of salt.
- [ ] 4 cups Frozen Swimming Scallops in shell
- [ ] 1/4 cup Each: Basil and Cilantro

## Instructions

1. Using a large pot or saucepan that has enough space for all your scallops, sauté onion, garlic and ginger in the vegetable oil until soft and fragrant.
2. Add in the curry paste, stir and cook 2-3 more minutes. Cooking the curry paste briefly helps freshen up and intensify the flavours. Regular coconut milk with clear and creamy parts is poured into red curry paste ready to mix.
3. Add in the coconut milk, lime juice, salt and sugar and simmer on low for 15 minutes. Adjust seasoning to taste at this point. (Add more salt, pepper, sugar or lime to balance the flavour further if desired.) Orange pink coconut curry broth simmering in a pot with a black spoon in it.
4. Rinse excess frozen seawater glaze off scallops if required and add to pot while still frozen. Stir and put lid on.
5. Cook 5 minutes (until most scallops have opened).
6. Sprinkle basil and cilantro over and serve immediately with rice or naan bread. Overhead shot of red Thai curry scallops in grey wide bowl on a marble worktop with silver soup spoon and flatbread on the side.

## Notes

Rinsing the scallops - It is important to rinse the seawater glaze off the scallops before adding them in so they don't water down your sauce and make it too salty. A quick rinse under cold running water right before adding is all they need, you don't want them to thaw all the way through.

## Substitutions

- Herbs - If you are missing basil or cilantro, add mint, spring onions or parsley instead.
- Coconut Milk - This recipe is made with regular coconut milk, but if you prefer you can use light coconut milk or coconut cream. This will make the broth thinner or heavier, but the flavour will still be delicious.
- Dairy Cream -If you don't have coconut milk or simply don't like it, you can use 2 cups of heavy cream, and one cup of milk in place of the coconut milk.

## Variations

- Green Scallop Curry – Swap an equal amount of Thai green curry paste for Thai red curry paste for a green coconut curry that is more basil flavoured than chilli flavoured.
- Spicy – Like your curry authentically spicy? Add in a teaspoon to a whole tablespoon of hot chilli sauce (sriracha or sambal oelek) at the same time as your curry paste. Alternatively, serve the hot sauce on the side for each guest to heat up their own as they like.
- Madras Curried Scallops – Use an equal amount of madras curry paste or 1 tablespoon of yellow curry powder added instead of the red curry paste for an Indian take on scallop curry.

## Storage

This will keep well up to 2 days in the fridge, but is truly best eaten fresh right when ready. This will not freeze well.

## Make Ahead

While you cannot cook your scallops ahead of time, you can make your curry ahead. Simply make until it is ready to add the scallops, then cool and refrigerate up to 2 days. When ready to finish, bring to a simmer on medium, add scallops and finish with your herbs.
