## Ingredients

- [ ] 3 large chicken breasts
- [ ] ¼ cup oregano, roughly chopped
- [ ] 1 tablespoon all-purpose (plain) flour
- [ ] ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic
- [ ] 1 fresh mild red chilli
- [ ] 3–4 anchovies
- [ ] ¾ cup dry white wine
- [ ] 16 pitted black Kalamata olives
- [ ] 16 cherry tomatoes
- [ ] Salt and pepper
- [ ] 1 handful of chopped flat-leaf parsley

## Instructions

1. Cut chicken into bite size pieces and place on your cutting board. Season with salt and pepper, sprinkle with dried oregano and coat in flour. Set aside, spread out so they are not touching.
2. Cut the tomatoes and olives in half.
3. Peel the three garlic cloves and slice two of the cloves very finely.
4. Chop the parsley finely and grate the remaining garlic clove into it. Mix well.
5. Add olive oil to a frying pan over medium heat. Once very hot, add the chicken pieces. If your pan is too small, do in two batches. (Chicken should almost be cooked through but not quite)
6. Once all pieces are nice and brown on all sides, push to the top of the pan and add the chilli, sliced garlic and anchovies, sweat for 30 seconds as you break away the anchovies with a wooden spoon. Deglaze pan with white wine and bring to a simmer. Add in olives and cherry tomatoes. Cook for another 2–3 minutes as you mix or toss gently.
7. Add the finely chopped garlic and parsley mixture at the last second to retain that freshness. Mix a few seconds and remove from heat. Leave chicken to rest in pan for 2–3 minutes before serving.

## Notes

###### _One Pan Healthy Mediterranean Chicken with Cherry Tomatoes and Olives_

This Mediterranean chicken, is not only a delicious dish but also packs a punch in terms of health benefits.  If you like one pan meals, then this is for you.  Plus, it’s ready in just 20 minutes, making it perfect for busy weeknights.

Now, for those who don’t like anchovies. While some might be hesitant to use them, they actually contribute a lot to the dish. Let me explain! Anchovies add a subtle umami flavour, that enhances the overall taste of the chicken without making it taste fishy. Umami is often described as the savoury or meaty taste, and it’s one of the five basic tastes alongside sweet, sour, bitter, and salty. So don’t be scared to use them.

In terms of health benefits, this dish is packed with protein from the chicken breasts, which helps in muscle growth and repair. The extra virgin olive oil used in cooking is rich in monounsaturated fats, which are heart-healthy and can help reduce the risk of heart disease. So use a good one, don’t buy the cheap fake ones in your supermarket. (See my video on buying good olive oil)

The garlic and chilli make the dish tasty and help fight off germs and reduce inflammation. Garlic is like a superhero for your immune system and can even help with blood pressure and cholesterol. Cherry tomatoes give you vitamins and antioxidants that keep you healthy. And olives bring in good fats and more antioxidants, especially vitamin E. So, not only is this delicious Mediterranean chicken quick and easy, but it’s also excellent for you. So, enjoy it today!
