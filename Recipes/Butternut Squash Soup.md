## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 tablespoon olive oil
- [ ] 1 chopped onion
- [ ] 1 cup sliced carrots
- [ ] 6 whole garlic cloves, peeled
- [ ] 2 teaspoons kosher salt, or to taste
- [ ] 1 (3 1/2) pound butternut squash, halved lengthwise and seeded
- [ ] 3 cups water
- [ ] 4 cubes chicken bouillon
- [ ] ½ teaspoon dried marjoram
- [ ] ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
- [ ] ⅛ teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
- [ ] 2 (8-ounce) packages of cream cheese

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
2. Drizzle olive oil into a large roasting pan. Add onion, carrots, garlic cloves, and a pinch of salt. Toss until coated in oil. Place squash halves flesh side up on the chopped vegetables. Sprinkle squash with additional salt.
3. Roast in preheated oven until squash is tender and easily pierced with the tip of a knife, about 1 hour. Allow vegetables to cool a bit.
4. Scoop out squash flesh and place in a roasting pan with roasted chopped vegetables.
5. Transfer mixture into a large stockpot, add water, bouillon, marjoram, black pepper and cayenne pepper. Bring to boil; cook 20 minutes.
6. Purée squash and cream cheese in a blender or food processor in batches until smooth. Return to saucepan, and heat through. Do not allow it to boil.

### Notes

- Instead of cream cheese, I tried adding sour cream to each bowl of soup served; very good!
