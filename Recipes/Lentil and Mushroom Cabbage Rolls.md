## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 savoy cabbage
- [ ] 4oz | 120g | 1.5 cup cooked brown lentils
- [ ] 1 chopped onion
- [ ] 2 chopped garlic cloves
- [ ] 8oz | 230g | 3 cups chopped mushrooms
- [ ] 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- [ ] 1 teaspoon miso paste
- [ ] 1 teaspoon paprika
- [ ] 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast (optional)

## Instructions

1. Take leaves of the cabbage and place in a large deep bowl. Then cover with boiling water and let stand for 15 minutes.
2. Sauté chopped onions and garlic cloves for 3 minutes.
3. Chop 3 cups of mushrooms and add to the frying-pan and cook for 6–8 minutes.
4. Add soy sauce, tomato paste, miso paste, paprika, and nutritional yeast if using. Stir to mix well. Add the 1.5 cups of cooked brown lentils and stir to combine. Remove from the heat and set aside.
5. Remove the cabbage from the water and remove the large veins from each leaf.
6. Add a couple spoonfuls for the mushroom and lentil mixture to each leaf and roll them up.
7. Place in a frying-pan, cover and cook them over medium heat for 5–7 minutes.
8. Flip them and cook for a further 5–7 minutes.
9. Cover a tahini dressing and serve.

## Notes

- I am sure these could be cooked in the oven as well.
- Feel free to add your favourite herbs and spices to the recipe to get the flavour you enjoy.
- You can substitute Savoy cabbage for Napa cabbage or a regular cabbage.
- You can substitute lentils for quinoa, mung beans, rice or other grains you enjoy.
