## Ingredients

- [ ] 1-2 green onions
- [ ] 1/3 carrot
- [ ] 2 tablespoons neutral-tasting oil
- [ ] 1 large egg
- [ ] 1 cup (210g) cooked and cooled rice, preferably day-old
- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce
- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon oyster sauce
- [ ] 6-7 frozen shrimp, thawed and patted dry
- [ ] 1 teaspoon fish sauce
- [ ] Handful of microgreens (or salad greens), to serve

## Instructions

1. Thinly slice the green onions. Cut the carrot into small pieces.
2. In a wok (or pan), heat the oil (2 tablespoons) over **medium-high heat**. Once it gets nice and hot, add the green onions. Sauté for **1 minute**, or until fragrant. Crack an egg into the wok and stir it quickly.
3. Once the egg is cooked, add the rice. Increase the heat to **high**. Mix to combine.
4. Push the rice to one side and, into the empty space, add the soy sauce. Wait for a second for that smoky umami flavour and mix them all together.
5. Add the oyster sauce and give it a few stirs. Add the carrot and shrimp. (If you’re using raw shrimp, blanch it and pat dry before you add it to the wok.) Cook until the shrimp is almost done cooking.
6. Add the fish sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry it for **another 1–2 minutes**.
7. Place on a serving plate. Serve it with any greens you like. Enjoy!
