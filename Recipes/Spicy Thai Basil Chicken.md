## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 lb (ca. 454 g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, thinly sliced or minced
- [ ] 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- [ ] 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 3-4 Thai bird chillies, finely chopped (adjust for spice level)
- [ ] 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- [ ] 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- [ ] 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- [ ] 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce (optional, for color)
- [ ] 1 teaspoons sugar
- [ ] 1/4 cup chicken broth or water
- [ ] 1/2 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
- [ ] Cooked jasmine rice, for serving
- [ ] Lime wedges, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Thinly slice or mince the chicken thighs or breasts, depending on your preference.
2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying-pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and Thai bird chilies, and sauté for 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant, but be careful not to burn the garlic.
3. Add the chicken to the frying-pan and stir-fry for 5–7 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly browned.
4. Add the sliced onion and red bell pepper to the frying-pan and stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce (if using), sugar, and chicken broth. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring to coat everything evenly. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
6. Turn off the heat and stir in the fresh Thai basil leaves. Toss until the basil wilts, about 1 minute.
7. Serve the Spicy Thai Basil Chicken over jasmine rice. Garnish with lime wedges for a zesty kick if desired.
