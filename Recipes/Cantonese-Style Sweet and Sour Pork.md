## Ingredients

### For the Sauce

- [ ] 1 cup orange juice (from about 4 oranges)
- [ ] 1 cup (3 ounces (ca. 113 g)) pineapple chunks, fresh or canned, preferably in juice, drained
- [ ] 1/2 red bell pepper (3 ounces (ca. 113 g)), seeded and roughly chopped
- [ ] 1/2 small onion (2 ounces (ca. 76 gram)), roughly chopped
- [ ] 3 tablespoons white wine vinegar
- [ ] 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
- [ ] 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- [ ] 1 tablespoon ketchup
- [ ] 6 tablespoons water
- [ ] 3 tablespoons cornstarch

### For the Pork

- [ ] Peanut oil for frying
- [ ] 1 large egg, lightly beaten
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon fine salt
- [ ] 1/2 cup cornstarch
- [ ] 10 to 12 ounces (ca. 454 g) pork loins, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
- [ ] Cooked rice, for serving (see [[Cantonese-Style Sweet and Sour Pork#Notes|Notes]])

## Instructions

1. To make the sauce: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, stir together the juice, pineapple, bell pepper, onion, vinegar, sugar, tomato paste and ketchup, until well combined and bring to a boil, stirring occasionally. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 5 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, in a small measuring cup, whisk together the water and cornstarch until combined. Slowly add the cornstarch slurry to the sauce, a little at a time, stirring continuously, until the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Cover and remove from the heat.
3. Make the pork: Line a plate with a towel and place it near the stove.
4. Pour enough oil into a large saucepan for the pork nuggets to float, and heat over medium-high heat until the oil registers 340 degrees on an instant-read thermometer. (If you don’t have a thermometer, test the oil by dropping in a small bit of cornstarch; if it immediately sizzles, the oil is ready.)
5. While the oil preheats, set out two shallow bowls. In one, whisk together the egg and salt; in the other, add the cornstarch. Add the pork to the egg and toss until each piece is well coated. Then, add the pork to the bowl of cornstarch, tossing until well coated.
6. When the oil is ready, use a slotted spoon to lower the pork into the oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through, about 5 minutes. (The internal temperature should be 170 degrees.) Remove the pork and drain on the prepared plate.
7. Arrange the pork on a serving platter, spoon the sauce over it and serve with the rice on the side.

## Notes

The amount of uncooked rice you’ll need to yield about 2 cups of cooked rice will vary depending on the variety of rice you choose. For long-grain white rice, rinse 2/3 cup of rice until the water runs clear. Then, place it in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add 1/2 teaspoon of olive oil and stir to coat the rice. Add 1 1/3 cups of water, a pinch of salt, if desired, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, 10 to 15 minutes.
