## Ingredients

### For the Acorn Squash

- [ ] 2 acorn squash, bottoms removed and cut in half
- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil per half of the acorn squash
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon coarse sea salt per half acorn squash
- [ ] 1 twist fresh cracked black pepper per acorn squash half

### For the Stuffing

- [ ] 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
- [ ] 2/3 cup diced yellow onion
- [ ] 2 large cloves minced garlic
- [ ] 3/4 cup cranberries
- [ ] 1 lb (ca. 454 g) mild Italian Sausage, uncased
- [ ] 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
- [ ] 2 cups fresh spinach
- [ ] 4-6 oz smoked Gouda cheese
- [ ] For serving: Additional cheese, fresh parsley and coarse sea salt and fresh cracked black pepper.

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 400. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet and brush with extra virgin olive oil. Add the salt and pepper to each half and then flip them over. When the oven is preheated, roast them for 30 minutes.
2. About 15 minutes before the acorn squash is done, start the sausage mix. Over medium heat in a large pan on the stove, add the extra virgin olive oil. Next add the onions and garlic and cook for 3–5 minutes, stirring frequently. Next add in the Italian Sausage and brown it.
3. Once the sausage has browned, reduce the heat to low and add in the walnuts, cranberries, and spinach. Add the spinach a little at a time if you have to until it wilts. Let everything simmer together on low for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Then turn off the heat.
4. Give the pan a few minutes to cool and mix in the cheese. If you have someone who does not love cheese (my husband) you can portion theirs out before adding in the cheese.
5. Carefully flip the acorn squash halves back over and stuff the centre of them with the sausage mix. Then put everything back in the oven for 5 minutes.
6. To serve, place the acorn squash halves on a plate and top with additional cheese, parsley, and salt and pepper to taste. I never feel like I need a lot of extra salt on these due to the flavour of the sausage so make sure to taste it first before seasoning your own portion.
