## Ingredients

- [ ] 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
- [ ] 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- [ ] 1 tablespoon honey
- [ ] 1 garlic clove, grated
- [ ] 1 teaspoon salt
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- [ ] 1.5 cups avocado oil or neutral oil

## Instructions

1. In a blender or container for your immersion blender, add the balsamic vinegar, mustard, honey, garlic, salt, and Italians seasoning. Blend until mixed.
2. While blending, slowly add the oil a little at a time to emulsify. Add until you reach desired thickness. Serve or store in the fridge for up to 3 weeks.

## Notes

- **Balsamic Vinegar** – now it wouldn’t be a balsamic vinaigrette without a balsamic vinegar! I love using balsamic from Modena Italy. This is where the best balsamic vinegars come from! Many are sold in everyday grocery stores too.
- **Dijon Mustard** – the mustard here is used for flavour and also to help emulsify the dressing and make it creamy. You may omit the mustard if you don’t like mustard. Adding an egg yolk in place would work great for the emulsion.
- **Honey** – I use honey as the sweetener here. You can use any sugar of your choice!
- **Garlic** – just one garlic clove is enough for this whole dressing. It isn’t too overpowering, and adds a depth of flavour. If you dislike garlic, you can omit the garlic clove.
- **Italian seasoning** – this is used to flavour the dressing. You can use any dried herb that you like here in place of Italian seasoning. I love rosemary, sage, and thyme in this recipe.
- **Avocado oil** – I like using a neutral oil for salad dressings because it won’t taste bitter or have a flavour. Many oils have too strong of a flavour for vinaigrettes. If you use an olive oil, make sure you use a high quality EVOO, or it WILL BE BITTER!
