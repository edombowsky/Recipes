## Ingredients

- [ ] 5 to 6 lbs beef chuck roast (I used 5.5 lbs)

### Seasoning Rub

- [ ] Salt and pepper to your taste (I used 2 t salt and 1/2 tablespoons pepper)
- [ ] 2 teaspoons oregano
- [ ] 1 teaspoon cumin
- [ ] 1 teaspoon chilli powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon onion powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon garlic powder

### Vegetables

- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil to sauté veggies
- [ ] 1 large onion, cut up
- [ ] 3 stalks of celery
- [ ] 4 large carrots, cut up
- [ ] 6 cloves of garlic, minced
- [ ] salt and pepper to season veggies

### Sauce

- [ ] 2 to 3 tablespoons oil to sear meat
- [ ] 2 tablespoons butter to make sauce
- [ ] 3 tablespoons flour
- [ ] 1/2 cup red wine, or chicken broth
- [ ] 2 cups chicken broth
- [ ] 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- [ ] 5 tablespoons tomato paste
- [ ] 4 tablespoons brown sugar

### Slow-cooker

- [ ] 3 bay leaves
- [ ] fresh thyme (I did not measure, but about 1 handful)
- [ ] 4 to 5 sprigs of rosemary

## Instructions

1. Season the roast with the seasoning of salt, pepper, oregano, cumin, chilli powder, onion powder, and garlic powder.
2. Set meat aside while prepping the vegetables. Chop into fairly large chunks and mince the garlic.
3. Drizzle cast-iron pan with olive oil. Once it is hot add the onion, celery, and carrots. Season them with some salt and pepper. Sauté them for them on medium heat for about 5 minutes. Now add the minced garlic and sauté for a further 30 seconds or until it is fragrant.
4. Add the sautéd vegetables to the slow cooker.
5. Using the same pan that the vegetables were sautéd in add some more oil and sear the meat on both sides for about 3 minutes per side or until each is a nice brown crust.
6. Take the meat from the frying pan and place on the bed of vegetables in the slow cooker.
7. In the frying pan used to sear the meat add some butter until it melts, The add the all-purpose flour and cook for about a minute to cook out the flour taste. Add the red wine and stir to incorporate well.
8. Add the chicken broth and stir until well mixed and then cook for about a minute until the sauce starts to thicken. Now add in the Worcestershire sauce, tomato paste and sugar stirring until well incorporated.
9. Simmer the sauce for about one minute for the flavours to come together.
10. Pour the sauce over the meat and top with some bay leaves and fresh herbs.
11. Cook the roast on low for 8–10 hours. Remove the fresh herbs as they are no longer required and let the meat sit for 10 to 15 minutes before serving. While waiting there is time to skim off the rendered fat.
