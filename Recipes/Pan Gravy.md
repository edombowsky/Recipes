## Ingredients

- [ ] Fat drippings from roast chicken, or 3 tablespoons of butter
- [ ] 1/2 onion, diced
- [ ] 1 carrot, diced
- [ ] 1 celery rib, diced
- [ ] 1.5 tablespoons all-purpose flour or gluten-free flour
- [ ] 1/2 cup dry white wine, sauvignon blanc is my fave
- [ ] 3 sprigs of thyme
- [ ] 2 cups of chicken stock
- [ ] salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Add the drippings to a medium size pot and place the pot stovetop over medium heat.
2. Add onions, carrot, celery, and sauté until translucent, salting as you go. Add minced garlic and sauté another 2 minutes. Add flour and coat the vegetables evenly. Once coated, deglaze with white wine, stirring constantly to prevent clumping. Reduce until it is almost dry.
3. Add the remainder of the ingredients. Slowly whisk in the stock to prevent clumping. Season with salt and pepper, add herbs. Bring to a simmer and simmer for 15–20 minutes until thickened and the raw flour taste is gone.
4. Strain through a fine mesh strainer and serve! (alternatively, blend the vegetables into the gravy for more flavour instead of straining).

## Notes

- Drippings from the roast chicken or turkey – this helps flavour the gravy! If you don’t have drippings, use butter or oil in place of it.
- White wine – this isn’t in many pan gravy recipes, but I think it is essential to balance out the richness of gravy. I love a dry sauvignon blanc.
- Chicken stock – homemade is preferred, but honestly I didn’t even have time for it for this recipe, so I used store bought.
- Thyme and herbs – the best herbs for this are thyme, rosemary, or sage.
