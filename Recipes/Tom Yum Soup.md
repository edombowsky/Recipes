## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 teaspoon sugar
- [ ] 2 tablespoons fish sauce (adds savoury depth; can use soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
- [ ] 4 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian option)
- [ ] 1 stalk lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
- [ ] 3–4 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces
- [ ] 2–3 Thai bird’s eye chillies, smashed (adjust based on spice preference)
- [ ] 1-inch piece of galangal (or ginger), sliced
- [ ] 1/2 lb shrimp or chicken, peeled and deveined
- [ ] 1 cup mushrooms, sliced (shiitake or white mushrooms work well)
- [ ] 1 tablespoon lime juice (adjust to taste)
- [ ] 1–2 teaspoons Thai chilli paste (Name Prik Pao) (optional for extra heat and flavour)

### **For Garnish and Serving**

- [ ] Fresh cilantro, chopped
- [ ] Fresh lime wedges

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the Broth

1. In a large pot, pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring it to a gentle boil over medium heat.
2. Add the smashed lemongrass, torn kaffir lime leaves, smashed bird’s eye chillies, sliced galangal, and minced garlic.
3. Reduce the heat to a simmer, allowing the aromatic herbs to infuse the broth. Let it cook for 5–7 minutes.

### Step 2: Add Mushrooms and Protein

1. Add the sliced mushrooms to the broth and let them cook for about 2 minutes.
2. Carefully add the shrimp or chicken to the pot, and cook for 3–5 minutes, or until the shrimp turn pink and are fully cooked (or until the chicken is tender).

### Step 3: Season the Soup

1. Add the fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar to the pot.
2. If you want additional heat and depth, stir in 1–2 teaspoons of Thai chilli paste (Name Prik Pao).
3. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with extra lime juice or fish sauce to reach your preferred balance of spicy, sour, and salty flavours.

### **Step 4: Serve**

1. Remove the lemongrass and galangal pieces from the pot to prevent bitterness.
2. Ladle the soup into serving bowls.
3. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro.
4. Optionally, serve with lime wedges for extra sourness.

## Notes

Tom Yum Soup is traditionally served as an appetizer or main dish in Thai cuisine. It pairs excellently with:

- Steamed Jasmine Rice: This helps balance the soup’s strong flavours and adds a filling element.
- Thai Salad: A light, refreshing salad can complement the soup’s spiciness.
- Stir-Fried Vegetables: Adding an assortment of lightly seasoned vegetables provides textural variety.
- Spring Rolls: Fried or fresh spring rolls add a delightful crunch that pairs well with the soup’s tangy, spicy profile.

### Variations of the Recipe

#### Tom Yum Goong (Shrimp Tom Yum)

- This classic version uses shrimp as the main protein and includes a touch of Thai chilli paste for extra flavour.

#### 2. Chicken Tom Yum

- Swap the shrimp for chicken to create a heartier, milder flavour. Chicken pairs well with the citrus notes from lime and lemongrass.

#### 3. Vegetarian Tom Yum

- Use vegetable broth and replace fish sauce with soy sauce or coconut aminos for a vegan-friendly option. Add tofu for protein if desired.

#### 4. Coconut Tom Yum (Tom Kha)

- Adding coconut milk creates a creamy, rich variation called Tom Kha, which is slightly less spicy and has a more mellow, comforting taste.

### FAQs

Q: Can I make Tom Yum Soup ahead of time?  
A: Yes, the broth can be prepared ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to two days. Reheat and add fresh shrimp or chicken before serving for the best taste.

Q: Can I freeze Tom Yum Soup?  
A: Tom Yum Soup freezes well; however, avoid freezing with shrimp, as it can change the texture. Freeze the broth, then add fresh shrimp or chicken upon reheating.

Q: How can I make the soup less spicy?  
A: Adjust the spice level by using fewer bird’s eye chillies, or omit them entirely if you prefer a milder version.

Q: What can I use instead of Thai bird’s eye chillies?  
A: Substitute with red pepper flakes or a milder chilli, like jalapeño, for a less intense heat.
