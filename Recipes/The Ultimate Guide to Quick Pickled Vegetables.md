## Ingredients

### Pickling Brine

- [ ] 1 cup hot water 236 mL
- [ ] 2 teaspoons non iodized salt*
- [ ] 1 cup vinegar apple cider or white vinegar, 236 mL

##### Pickled Cauliflower

- [ ] 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
- [ ] 2 cloves of garlic crushed
- [ ] Pinch crushed red pepper flakes
- [ ] 2 cups chopped cauliflower

##### Pickled Green Beans

- [ ] 5 to 10 sprigs fresh dill about 1 to 2 teaspoons if chopped
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic crushed
- [ ] ½ lb green beans trimmed, 226 g

##### Pickled Beets

- [ ] 1 teaspoon sugar
- [ ] 1 bay leaf
- [ ] 1 clove garlic crushed
- [ ] 1 to 2 beets diced or cut into half moons

## Instructions

1. **Brine**: Heat water in either a kettle, the microwave, or on the stove until steaming hot. Stir in salt until dissolved. Stir in vinegar.
2. **Pick Your Pickle**: Choose your flavour, then add all the ingredients to a lidded non-reactive container (like a glass jar or ceramic vessel). Pour liquid over ingredients so that they are covered (you may not need all the liquid).
3. **Pickle**: Let cool to room temperature, then seal shut and transfer to the refrigerator. (Let pickle for at least 1 hour before digging in.)

## Notes

- *Non iodized* salt should be used when pickling. Iodized salt can cause your pickled products to turn a dark colour.
- Store in the refrigerator for 2 to 3 weeks.
- Not tested for long-term canning storage. Be sure to follow the best USDA canning practices if canning for long-term storage.
- More pickling inspiration: Jalapeños, Cucumbers, Radishes, Grapes, Red Onions, Cabbage.

### Ideas To Get You Started

Here are some of the pickled foods that I’ve created. They’re all very easy to make, with each one requiring right around 5 minutes of hands-on Prep_Time.

**[Cauliflower](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/pickled-cauliflower/)**: Pickled cauliflower utilizes 1 cup hot water, 2 teaspoons non iodized salt, 1 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar, 2 cups chopped cauliflower, 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns, 2 cloves crushed garlic, and a pinch crushed red pepper flakes.

**Green Beans**: To make pickled green beans, you’ll need 1 cup hot water, 2 teaspoons non iodized salt, 1 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar, ½ lb (226 g) trimmed green beans, 5 to 10 sprigs (1 to 2 teaspoons chopped) fresh dill, and 2 cloves crushed garlic.

**Beets:** Pickled beets involve using 1 cup hot water, 2 teaspoons non iodized salt, 1 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar, 1 to 2 beets (**diced or cut into half moons**), a single teaspoon sugar, 1 bay leaf, and 1 clove of crushed garlic.

**[Jalapeños](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/quick-pickled-jalapenos/)**: To make pickled jalapeños, you’ll need 1 cup hot water, 2 teaspoons sugar, 2 teaspoons non iodized salt, 1 cup white vinegar, 10 to 15 jalapeños, 2 cloves crushed garlic, 1 bay leaf, a pinch cumin, and a pinch oregano. You can make these less spicy by removing some jalapeño seeds.

**[Radishes](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/quick-pickled-radishes/)**: Pickled radishes require 1 cup hot water, 2 tablespoons sugar, 2 teaspoons non iodized salt, ¾ cup white wine vinegar, ½ lb of radishes, 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, 1 clove of crushed garlic, 1 bay leaf, ½ teaspoon of crushed red pepper, and ½ teaspoon coriander seeds.

**[Cucumbers](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/refrigerator-pickles/)**: Refrigerator pickles will require 2 pickling cucumbers (or 1 English cucumber), 1 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar, 1 cup water, 1 teaspoon non iodized salt, 10 sprigs (2 teaspoons chopped) fresh dill, 4 cloves crushed garlic, 1 bay leaf, 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns, and a pinch crushed red pepper flakes.

**[Onions](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/quick-pickled-red-onions/)**: Pickled onions simply require ½ cup hot water, 1 tablespoon sugar or honey, 1 teaspoon non iodized salt, ½ apple cider vinegar, and 1 large thinly sliced red onion.

**[Cabbage](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/easy-pickled-red-cabbage/)**: To make pickled cabbage, you will need ¼ of a red [cabbage (sliced)](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/how-to-cut-cabbage/), ½ cup apple cider vinegar or red wine vinegar, ½ cup water, 1 tablespoon sugar, 1 clove minced garlic, 1 teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper.

**[Grapes](https://www.liveeatlearn.com/quick-pickled-grapes/)**: For pickled grapes (and most fruits), you will need 1 cup hot water, ½ cup sugar, 1 cup apple cider vinegar, 2 inches (ca. 5 centimetres) peeled and thinly sliced ginger, 1 tablespoon coriander seeds, 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns, 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, 2 cinnamon sticks, 1 bay leaf, and 2 to 3 cups of seedless red grapes.
