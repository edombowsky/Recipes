## Ingredients

### Tofu Ground Beef

- [ ] 1 package (16 oz) SUPER FIRM (or Extra Firm) tofu

### Marinade

- [ ] 1 teaspoon Beefless Broth (or Veggie Broth Concentrate)
- [ ] 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce
- [ ] 1 teaspoon marmite (or 1 tablespoon Steak Sauce)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon liquid smoke
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Browning Sauce (or 1 teaspoon Vegan Worcestershire)
- [ ] 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- [ ] 2 tablespoons neutral oil
- [ ] 2 tablespoons water
- [ ] 1 heaping tablespoons of tomato paste (optional for more umami)
- [ ] 1 tablespoons Blackstrap molasses (optional for colour and flavour!)

## Instructions

1. Remove tofu from packaging and pat dry. You can press the tofu if you wish, but I didn't find it necessary. Crumble into a bowl.
2. Make the marinade by combining everything in a bowl. Pour over the crumbled tofu. With clean hands, toss the crumbles and coat thoroughly in the marinade.
3. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper / cooking spray and spread out the tofu pieces in a single layer. Bake at 350º F / 175º C for 20–30 minutes, or until the tofu is slightly dried out and chewy. Extra Firm tofu may need an extra 10 minutes, so 30–40. I would suggest checking it every 15 minutes if you're unsure so you don't dry it out too much.
4. Transfer the tofu to a 12-inch skillet, and add 1 cup of liquid (either water, broth, or a mixture, depending on how salty you want the meat, I recommend tasting the tofu before adding any broth so it doesn't get too salty) and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and then simmer until the liquid is mostly reduced. About 10 minutes or so. Taste and adjust for seasoning.
5. Your meat is done! Enjoy as you wish! Like I said, it can be used in just about any application you would use regular ground beef!

## Notes

- Store any leftovers in the fridge and eat within 3 – 5 days, or freeze for up to 2 months.
