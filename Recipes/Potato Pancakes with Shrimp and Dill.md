## Ingredients

- [ ] 3 potatoes (1 1/4 lb / 625g total)
- [ ] 3 eggs
- [ ] 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
- [ ] 1 teaspoon baking powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon salt
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] 3 tablespoons butter, melted
- [ ] 1 1/3 cups of light cream
- [ ] 1 cup cooked baby shrimp
- [ ] 3 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
- [ ] 1/3 cup (approx.) unsalted butter

## Instructions

1. Peel potatoes and cut them into quarters; cook in a saucepan of boiling salted water until tender.
2. Drain well and mash until smooth to make 2 cups (500 mL).
3. Transfer to a bowl and let cool.
4. In a separate bowl, beat eggs; blend in flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
5. Whisk in melted butter and cream; gradually whisk into cool mashed potatoes.
6. Pat shrimp very dry; stir into potato mixture along with dill.
7. In a large frying-pan, melt 1 tablespoon (15 mL) of the unsalted butter over medium heat; pour in rounded tablespoonfuls (15 mL) of batter for each pancake, about 4 inches (10 cm) apart.
8. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes, or until tiny bubbles appear on the surface and the underside is golden.
9. Turn and cook for 1 to 1-1/2 minutes longer, or until golden.
10. Transfer pancakes to a platter and keep warm in a 150 °F (65° C) oven. Repeat with remaining butter and batter.

## Notes

- Pancakes can be covered and refrigerated for up to 1 day. To reheat, bake in a single layer on a baking sheet in a 400° F (200° C) oven for 4 to 5 minutes, or until heated.
