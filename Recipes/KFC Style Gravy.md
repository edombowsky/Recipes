## Ingredients

- [ ] 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- [ ] 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- [ ] 2 cups chicken broth (preferably low-sodium)
- [ ] 1 cup beef broth (for a rich flavour)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce (optional, for a touch of umami)

## Instructions

1. **Make the Roux**: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Once melted, add the flour and whisk constantly for about 2–3 minutes until the mixture turns a light golden brown. This is the base (roux) for your gravy.
2. **Add the Broth**: Gradually whisk in the chicken broth and beef broth, ensuring there are no lumps. Continue whisking until the mixture is smooth.
3. **Season the Gravy**: Stir in the onion powder, garlic powder, black pepper, salt, and soy sauce (if using). Keep whisking to combine all the flavours.
4. **Simmer and Thicken**: Bring the gravy to a simmer and let it cook for about 5–7 minutes, whisking occasionally, until it thickens to your desired consistency. The gravy should be smooth and velvety.
5. **Adjust Seasoning**: Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more salt, pepper, or soy sauce if needed.
6. **Serve**: Pour the hot, rich gravy over mashed potatoes, fried chicken, or any of your favourite comfort foods.

## Notes

- **Smooth Gravy**: Strain the gravy through a fine-mesh sieve if you want an extra smooth texture.
- **Make It Richer**: For an even deeper flavour, add a splash of heavy cream or a tablespoon of butter before serving.
- **Storage**: Store leftover gravy in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of broth if needed to thin it out.
