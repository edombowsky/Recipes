## Ingredients

- [ ] 1/2 tablespoon olive oil
- [ ] 1 small onion, minced
- [ ] 1 clove garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 celery stalk, minced
- [ ] 1/4 cup minced parsley
- [ ] 1/2 pound 93% lean ground turkey
- [ ] 1/2 pound 95% lean minced beef
- [ ] 1 large egg
- [ ] 1/4 cup seasoned breadcrumbs or gluten-free crumbs
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- [ ] 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon allspice
- [ ] 2 cups reduced sodium beef broth
- [ ] 3 ounces (ca. 113 g) reduced fat cream cheese
- [ ] chopped parsley, for garnish

## Instructions

1. In a large non-stick frying-pan, heat olive oil over medium heat and add onions and garlic. Sauté until onions are translucent, about 4–5 minutes. Add celery and parsley and cook until soft, about 3-4 more minutes. Set aside to cool.
2. In a large bowl, combine beef, turkey, egg, onion mixture, breadcrumbs, ¼ teaspoon salt, pepper, and allspice. Mix well and form into 16 meatballs.
3. Add beef broth to the pan and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and slowly drop meatballs into the broth. Cover and cook about 20 minutes, until cooked through.
4. Remove the meatballs with a slotted spoon and set aside in a serving dish. Strain the broth, transfer to a blender with cream cheese and blend until smooth. Return to the frying-pan with the meatballs and simmer.

## Notes

This makes 16 meatballs, nutritional info is based on 4 meatballs with gravy.
