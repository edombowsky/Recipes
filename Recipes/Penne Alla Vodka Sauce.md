## Ingredients

- [ ] 17.6 oz (500 g) penne pasta
- [ ] 1 × 28 oz (794 g) San Marzano tomatoes
- [ ] 3 tablespoons (45 mL) olive oil
- [ ] 1 shallot finely chopped
- [ ] 3/4 cup (180 mL) diced pancetta
- [ ] 3 cloves minced garlic
- [ ] Salt
- [ ] 1/2 cup (125 mL) vodka
- [ ] 1 teaspoon (5 mL) red pepper flakes more or less
- [ ] 2/3 cup (160 mL) heavy cream
- [ ] 1/2 cup (125 mL) grated parmigiano padano, plus more for serving
- [ ] 8 fresh basil leaves chopped

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large frying-pan add chopped shallot and pancetta, sauté 4–5 minutes, add garlic and red pepper flakes and sauté one minute longer.
2. Remove pan from heat and add blended (for a smoother sauce blend tomatoes) San Marzano tomatoes, vodka, season with salt. Return to medium-low heat, bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally and cook for about an hour and a half.
3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, add salt. Cook penne al dente according to package directions.
4. Once sauce is ready, stir in cream and just heat through about one minute. Stir in parmigiano padano and fresh chopped basil. Add drained penne to the frying-pan with the sauce, toss to coat.
5. Serve with additional parmigiano padano and more basil if desired.
