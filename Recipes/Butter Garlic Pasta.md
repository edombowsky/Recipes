## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 ounces (ca. 76 g) dried pasta
- [ ] 1 ½ cloves garlic
- [ ] 1 tablespoons butter
- [ ] ¼ teaspoon sea salt
- [ ] ⅛ teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] ⅛ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- [ ] ⅛ cup minced parsley

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high heat. Add the pasta and cook according to the package directions. Remove 1 cup of pasta water from the pot then drain the pasta then return it to the pot.
2. While the water is heating, make the garlic butter sauce. Mince 3 cloves of the garlic and slice the other 3 cloves.
3. Heat the butter in a small pan over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and cook for 2 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the salt and pepper.
4. After you return the pasta to the pot, pour the garlic butter sauce over the pasta and toss well to coat. Add the Parmesan cheese and a splash of the pasta water and toss the pasta again. Add just enough pasta water so that it creates a light sauce – you likely won't use it all. Mix in the minced parsley then serve it right away.

## Notes

### Garlic Butter Pasta Recipe Variations

Garlic, butter, and pasta are three of my favourite words, so you can't go wrong when you put them into one sentence/ dish! If you want to add any flavour variations, here are some of my top suggestions:

- **Protein boost:** The portion sizes of this recipe are meant to serve as a side dish. If you're making it a main, add some grilled chicken, shrimp, crumbly bacon, or prosciutto.
- **Veggie boost:** A handful of spinach added towards the end of the cooking process is an easy way to add greens. You can also throw in some roasted bell pepper slices, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, or mushrooms.
- **Herb infusion:** Add some Italian seasoning or include some extra fresh herbs. I like fresh basil.
- **Creamy garlic pasta:** Stir in a spoonful of ricotta to give it a velvety, creamy finish.
- **Spice it up:** A teaspoon of smoked paprika adds a beautiful smoky flavour to the butter and garlic. If you prefer a stronger kick, add a pinch of cayenne.
- **Lemon**: Brighten it with some lemon zest and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
