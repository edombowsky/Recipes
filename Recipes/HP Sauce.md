## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 cup malt vinegar
- [ ] 1/2 cup water
- [ ] 1/4 cup brown sugar
- [ ] 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- [ ] 2 teaspoons ground ginger
- [ ] 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon salt
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- [ ] 1/2 cup pitted dates, soaked in water for at least 4 hours

## Instructions

1. Drain and blend the soaked dates with a little water until smooth.
2. In a saucepan, combine malt vinegar, water, brown sugar, tomato paste, ginger, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, cloves, nutmeg, and the blended dates.
3. Whisk until smooth.
4. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10–15 minutes, or until thickened.
5. Remove from heat and let cool.
6. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
7. Let it cool and thicken before transferring to a glass bottle.
8. My updated recipe should yield a sauce that's even closer to the original HP sauce, with the added sweetness and depth of flavour from the dates.
