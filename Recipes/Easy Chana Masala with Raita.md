This quick and Easy Chana Masala recipe is loaded with hearty chickpeas and an Indian-spiced tomato onion sauce. A dollop of the refreshing vegan Raita (yoghurt sauce) on top cools every bite and makes this Indian-inspired dish extra indulgent.

Thick and hearty chana masala is a comforting menu item you’ll find at almost every Indian restaurant. Protein-packed chickpeas are cooked in a spicy tomato and onion sauce before being served with rice and [[Naan Bread]]. It’s always a filling and flavourful experience!

If you want to make a restaurant-worthy version at home, then give this quick and Easy Chana Masala recipe a try. Using a handful of budget and pantry-staple ingredients, you achieve the same great flavours and layers of spice in about 30 minutes!

One of my favourite ways to serve vegan chana masala is with a dollop of vegan Indian yoghurt sauce, or raita, on top. It’s a cooling Indian condiment made with yoghurt, cucumbers, and spices. Vegan raita is easy to make with store-bought yoghurt and cools down every bite of the spicy masala.

Chana masala is a North Indian curry dish. “Chana” is the Hindi word for “chickpeas” and “masala” means “spiced gravy”. Essentially, its chickpeas simmered in a spiced tomato onion sauce.

This recipe is quick and easy and not authentic, using pantry staples and served with vegan raita.

## Ingredients

### Chana Masala

- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
- [ ] 4 large cloves of garlic, minced
- [ ] 2 tablespoons fresh grated ginger
- [ ] 2 serrano peppers, finely diced optional
- [ ] 2 teaspoons garam masala
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper, or to taste
- [ ] 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- [ ] 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- [ ] 3/4 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
- [ ] 28 ounce can diced tomatoes with their juices
- [ ] (2) 15-ounce cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 1/2 cup water
- [ ] 2 teaspoons sugar
- [ ] 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
- [ ] 1 medium lemon, juice squeezed

### Vegan Raita

- [ ] 1 1/2 cups plain vegan yoghurt, I like cashew or coconut
- [ ] 1 cup finely diced English cucumber
- [ ] 1/4 cup finely chopped cilantro
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon salt

### For Serving

- [ ] [[Basmati Rice Pilaf]]
- [ ] [[Naan Bread]]

## Instructions

1. In a large pot, warm the oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for 3–4 minutes, until translucent.
2. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger and diced peppers, if using, and sauté for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add all the spices and salt and stir them into the onion mixture. Cook for about a minute, stirring constantly. Add a little more oil if it seems dry.
4. Pour in the diced tomatoes, chickpeas and the water. Stir well and bring to a boil, then lower heat to a simmer. Simmer, uncovered, for 20 minutes until it thickens somewhat. Stir occasionally to prevent anything sticking to the bottom of the pan.
5. Stir in the sugar, some chopped cilantro (save some for serving) and lemon juice. Taste; add more salt, cayenne pepper for heat or any other seasonings as desired.
6. **For the raita:** Combine the yoghurt, cucumber, cilantro, cumin and salt in a medium bowl and stir to combine.
7. Serve warm with basmati rice, vegan raita and perhaps some Vegan Naan. Sprinkle with a little cilantro, if desired. Enjoy!

## Notes

- It will keep in the refrigerator for up to 5 days in an airtight container.
- It freezes very well. Let it cool completely before freezing. Reheat on the stove top until warm.
- If you can, use Indian chilli powder instead of cayenne pepper.
- May also substitute crushed tomatoes or whole canned tomatoes for the diced.
