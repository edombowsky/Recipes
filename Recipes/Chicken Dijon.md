## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 tablespoons butter
- [ ] 4 to 6 boneless skinless chicken breasts (1 to 1 1/4 pounds total)
- [ ] salt and pepper
- [ ] 1 large onion chopped
- [ ] 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- [ ] 1 cup chicken stock
- [ ] 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- [ ] 1 tablespoon Dijon or coarse-grained mustard

## Instructions

1. In a heavy frying-pan, melt butter over medium-high heat; cook chicken until browned, about 3 minutes on each side, adding more butter if necessary. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste. Add onion and cook, stirring, until tender. Remove chicken from pan and keep warm.
2. Sprinkle flour over onion and cook, stirring, for 1 minute. Stir in stock and bring to a boil, stirring up brown bits from the bottom of pan; reduce heat, return chicken to pan and simmer for 3 to 5 minutes or until chicken is no longer pink inside (If using bone-in chicken, cook chicken in stock for 15 minutes or until no longer pink.) Remove from heat and stir in mustard.
