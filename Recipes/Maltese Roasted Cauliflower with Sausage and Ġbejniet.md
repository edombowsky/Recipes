## Ingredients

- [ ] 3 large ripe tomatoes or 400 g (14 oz) canned diced tomatoes
- [ ] 1 small onion, finely chopped
- [ ] 2 garlic cloves, finely sliced
- [ ] 1 teaspoon (4 ml) ground coriander
- [ ] 3 tablespoons (45 ml) extra-virgin olive oil
- [ ] 1 cup (250 ml) water
- [ ] pepper
- [ ] 1 small cauliflower (1 lb 2 oz - 1 lb 5 oz / 500-600 g) cut into florets
- [ ] 10 1/2 oz (300 g) Italian port sausages, cut into thirds
- [ ] 4 eggs
- [ ] 4 fresh Ġbejniet cheeslets or 12 1/2 oz (360 g) firm ricotta divided into 4 pieces
- [ ] 2 tablespoons (30 ml) chopped flat-leaf (Italian) parsley

## Instructions

1. If using fresh tomatoes, score them on their bases and grate with a box grater. Sauté the onion, garlic, and coriander in 1 tablespoon (15 ml) of the oil until soft. Add water and tomato and simmer for 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Pre-heat the oven to 375 F (190 C). Scoop the sauce into a deep, medium-sized baking dish. Scatter the cauliflower and sausage over the sauce, season with salt and pepper, and drizzle with another tablespoon of oil. Toss to coat the cauliflower and sausage in sauce. Cover well with foil and bake for 45 minutes, or until the cauliflower is soft.
3. Make spaces for 4 eggs and crack them into the wells. Return to the oven uncovered for a few minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your liking. Place a cheeselet or piece of ricotta in each corner of the dish and sprinkle parsley all over. Drizzle with the remaining oil and plonk the dish on the table. Enjoy with a salad and crusty bread.

## Notes

- The cauliflower may need a lot longer than 45 minutes in the oven to make it soft. Think another 45 minutes and with the last 30 uncovered and at 220° C not 190° C.
