## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 Cup / 200g Red Lentils (Soaked/Strained)
- [ ] 18g / 4 to 6 Garlic Cloves — Roughly chopped
- [ ] 8g / 1/2 Inch Ginger — Roughly chopped
- [ ] 140g / 1 cup Onions — chopped
- [ ] 60g / 1+1/2 cup Parsley — chopped & firmly packed
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Paprika
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Ground Cumin
- [ ] 2 teaspoon Ground Coriander
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon Ground Black Pepper
- [ ] 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon Cayenne Pepper (optional)
- [ ] Salt to taste (I added 1+1/4 Teaspoon of pink Himalayan salt)
- [ ] 180g / 1+1/2 cup (Firmly Packed) FINELY GRATED Carrots (2 to 3 Carrots)
- [ ] 80g / 3/4 Cup Toasted Rolled Oats
- [ ] 35g / 3/4 Cup Chickpea Flour or Besan
- [ ] 1 tablespoon Olive oil
- [ ] 2 tablespoon White Vinegar or White Wine Vinegar
- [ ] 1/4 Teaspoon Baking Soda

## Instructions

1. Wash the red lentil a few times, until the water runs clear. Then soak for 2 to 3 hours. Then drain the water and allow it to sit in a strainer until all the water is drained properly. This is an important step as it will prevent the patties from getting soggy.
2. Transfer the rolled oats to a pan and turn on the stove. Toast the rolled oats while switching between medium to medium-low heat for about 2 to 3 minutes or until it's lightly browned and fragrant. Be careful not to burn it. Once toasted *right away* transfer it to a plate, spread it out and allow it to cool down.
3. Finely grate the carrots using the finer side of the grater. Chop the onion, ginger, garlic, and parsley.
4. Transfer the soaked/strained red lentils to a food processor, add salt, paprika, ground cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, garlic, ginger, onions, parsley, and BLEND TO A COARSE MIXTURE. Make sure to scrape the sides of the container a few times in between so that it blends evenly. Then transfer the lentil mixture to a large bowl. Add finely grated carrots, toasted rolled oats, chickpea flour (or besan), baking soda, olive oil, white vinegar & mix well. Cover the bowl and let it rest for about 10 minutes — This is optional. Scoop the lentil mixture using a 1/4 cup (level it off by removing any excess mixture) and form equal size patties, about 1/2 inch thick. This recipe makes 16 lentil patties.
5. Heat up 2 to 3 tablespoons of oil in a pan and add the lentil patties 3 to 4 at a time, do not overcrowd the pan. Fry on medium heat for about 30 seconds. Then reduce the heat to medium-low and cook for 2 to 3 minutes or until the bottom is golden brown. Then flip the patties and cook for another 3 minutes on medium-low heat. Then increase the heat to medium and fry for about 20 to 30 seconds to get a golden brown crust. Be careful not to burn it. Remove the patties from the pan to a plate lined with paper towel. This will prevent the lentil patties from getting soggy and soak any excess oil.
6. Fry as many patties as you like and store the remainder of the lentil mixture in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3 to 4 days. This recipe is perfect for meal prep.

## Notes

- Cooking the patties on a lower heat allows the lentils to cook properly and evenly and prevent it from burning.

## Tahini Dip

- [ ] 1/2 cup Tahini
- [ ] 2 tablespoons Lemon Juice or to taste
- [ ] 1/ 3 to 1/2 cup Mayonnaise (Vegan)
- [ ] 1 to 2 Garlic Cloves — minced
- [ ] 1/4 to 1/2 Teaspoon Maple Syrup (optional)
- [ ] Salt to taste (I added 1/4 teaspoon pink Himalayan salt)
- [ ] 2 to 3 tablespoons ice water

1. Add all the ingredients to a bowl and whisk until the sauce is light and fluffy.
2. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator and use it through the week.
