## Ingredients

- [ ] 4 brioche buns
- [ ] 400g chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 400g haricot beans, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 1 carrot, peeled and grated
- [ ] 1 sweetheart cabbage, finely chopped
- [ ] 2 red onions, thinly sliced
- [ ] small handful dill, finely chopped
- [ ] salt
- [ ] pepper
- [ ] 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
- [ ] 1 tablespoons of natural yoghurt
- [ ] 1 teaspoon wholegrain mustard
- [ ] handful fresh coriander/parsley, roughly chopped
- [ ] 1 green chilli, finely chopped (optional)
- [ ] 2 slices stale sourdough
- [ ] 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
- [ ] oil of choice
- [ ] 4 tablespoons cornflour
- [ ] 4 tablespoons sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. To prepare the coleslaw, mix the carrot, cabbage, 1 red onion, dill, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon pepper, mayonnaise, yoghurt, and mustard in a large bowl. Cover and place it in the fridge whilst you prepare the burger.
2. Add the chickpeas and beans to a large bowl. Use a potato masher to crush them.
3. Place the sourdough in a blender and blitz into breadcrumbs. Transfer to the chickpea and bean mixture. Mix well.
4. Add the coriander/parsley, 1 red onion, green chilli, Thai red curry paste, and ¾ teaspoon salt. Mix well with your hands.
5. Take a large handful of mixture and form it into a patty. Repeat the process with the rest of the mixture.
6. Add the cornflour and 100ml of to a shallow bowl, and mix well to create a slurry. Pour the sesame seeds onto a plate.
7. Place a patty into the cornflour mixture and then shake gently to remove any excess before placing it onto the plate of sesame seeds to thoroughly coat the patty. Repeat the process with the other patties.
8. Add 3 tablespoons of oil to a hot pan. Add the patties and cook for 4–5 minutes on each side.
9. To assemble the burgers, add 2 tablespoons of coleslaw to the bottom half of the brioche bun and place a patty on top. Cover with the other half of the bun.
