A hearty **peasant pot** with minced meat and potatoes. This farm pot with potatoes and minced meat is basically nothing more than a hearty stew. A **classic family meal** that was probably cooked by Grandma. It tastes of home and is one of these dishes that you cook with love and where you can get out of a lot of taste with a few ingredients.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 500 g mixed minced meat (half beef and pork)
- [ ] 700 g potatoes
- [ ] 2 red peppers
- [ ] 150 g Zucchini
- [ ] 200 g carrots
- [ ] 1 onion
- [ ] 2 cloves of garlic
- [ ] 400 ml of tomatoes
- [ ] 400 ml beef broth, or vegetable broth
- [ ] 1 teaspoon marjoram, rake
- [ ] 1 teaspoon paprika powder
- [ ] 1 tablespoon paprika powder smoked
- [ ] 1 tablespoon thyme
- [ ] 1 pinch sugar
- [ ] Salt
- [ ] Black pepper
- [ ] 2 splashes Worcester sauce
- [ ] Oil to roast

## Instructions

1. First peel and cut the vegetables. The potatoes in bite-size pieces, the carrots in thin slices and the peppers and the zucchini also in small pieces / cubes. Finely dice the onions and the garlic.
2. Put on a large pan or a large saucepan and heat some oil in it. Fry the minced meat in it and cut it crumbly.
3. Use the heat down and add the onions and the garlic and sweat for 3–4 minutes. Salt and pepper.
4. Pour in the dosing tomatoes and the beef fund. Add the spices and about a 1/2 teaspoon of salt and stir well.
5. Then add the entire vegetables. Depending on the variety of the tomatoes, they are less liquid. Then “rinse” the can with approx. 100 ml of water and add to the hack. The vegetables should largely be covered by liquid.
6. Simmer everything for 20 minutes with a lid. Stir twice in between.
7. If the potatoes are soft, drop everything down again, round off Worcester sauce with a few splashes.
