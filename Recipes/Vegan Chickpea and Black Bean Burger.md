## Ingredients

### For the Burger Patties

- [ ] 1 can (15 ounces) of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 1 can (15 ounces) of black beans, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 1/2 cup rolled oats (gluten-free if needed)
- [ ] 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
- [ ] 1/2 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- [ ] 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon chilli powder (adjust to taste)
- [ ] Salt and black pepper to taste
- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil (for cooking)

### For Serving

- [ ] Whole-grain burger buns
- [ ] Lettuce leaves
- [ ] Sliced tomatoes
- [ ] Sliced red onions
- [ ] Vegan mayonnaise or your favourite vegan condiments

## Instructions

### Prepare the Burger Mixture

1. In a food processor, add the chickpeas and black beans. Pulse a few times until they are partially mashed but still have some texture. Don't over-process; you want some texture in the patties.
2. Transfer the mashed beans to a large mixing bowl.
3. Add the rolled oats, chopped onion, red bell pepper, minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, chilli powder, salt, and black pepper to the bowl.

### Mix and Shape Patties

1. Mix all the ingredients in the bowl until well combined. If the mixture seems too wet, you can add a bit more rolled oats.
2. Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape them into burger patties. You should be able to make about 4 to 6 patties, depending on your desired size.

### Cook the Patties

1. Heat the olive oil in a frying-pan over medium heat.
2. Once the oil is hot, add the patties to the frying-pan. Cook for about 4–5 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.

### Assemble the Burgers

1. Toast the burger buns lightly if desired.
2. Place a lettuce leaf on the bottom half of each bun.
3. Add a cooked burger patty on top of the lettuce.
4. Top with sliced tomatoes and red onions.
5. Spread vegan mayonnaise or your preferred condiments on the top half of the bun.
6. Place the top bun over the toppings to complete the burger.

### Serve

1. Serve your homemade vegan chickpea and black bean burgers hot with your favourite side dishes, such as baked sweet potato fries or a side salad.
