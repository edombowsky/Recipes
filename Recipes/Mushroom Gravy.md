Mushroom gravy is for everyone no matter if you're a vegetarian, meat eater, or just a serious mushroom fan. The creamy, flavourful gravy instantly elevates dishes of all kinds, and you don't need any meat drippings to make it!

## Ingredients

- [ ] 3 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 1 pound sliced mushrooms (Cremini, baby Bella, or white button)
- [ ] 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion or shallot
- [ ] 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon salt
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- [ ] 1 teaspoon chopped thyme leaves
- [ ] 2 tablespoons salted butter
- [ ] 1/4 cup flour
- [ ] 3 cups vegetable stock
- [ ] 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

## Instructions

1. Heat a large frying-pan over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil, then the mushrooms. Spread them in an even layer and let cook, undisturbed, for 4 minutes. Add the onion, garlic, salt, pepper, and thyme, and stir to combine. Cook 2 minutes more.
2. Reduce the heat to medium. Add the butter to the frying-pan. Once melted, sprinkle the flour evenly over the top of the mushrooms. Stir until the flour mixture is lightly toasted, about 1 minute.
3. Add the vegetable stock and soy sauce. Scrape the bottom of the frying-pan to remove all the brown bits and stir until smooth. Increase the heat to medium-high to return to a simmer, then reduce heat to medium and let the gravy simmer for 5 minutes.
4. Remove from the heat and stir in the parsley.

## Notes

- If you like a creamy gravy, stir in 2 tablespoons of heavy cream with the parsley.

**Can you make mushroom gravy ahead of time?**

Yes! Mushroom gravy will stay good for up to three days in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Reheat the gravy over low heat on the stove, stirring often, until warmed through. If the gravy looks like it has separated, just keep stirring until it comes back together.

**Can you freeze mushroom gravy?**

Yes! This gravy freezes well. Once it has cooled completely, place the gravy in an airtight container and freeze for up to three months. Let the gravy thaw in the refrigerator overnight, then reheat over low heat on the stove.
