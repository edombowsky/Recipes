This homemade sweet, sticky mango chutney recipe has a slight spiciness, It makes the perfect accompaniment for any curry!  
Making it yourself means you can control what goes into it, using lovely fresh ingredients and you can adjust the spiciness to suit your taste.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 teaspoon ghee or sunflower or olive oil
- [ ] 1 small onion finely diced
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic minced
- [ ] 2 teaspoons garam masala
- [ ] 1 pinch Aseofoetida powder *optional don't worry if you don't have it in your store cupboard*
- [ ] ½ teaspoon salt
- [ ] ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
- [ ] 1 teaspoon [nigella seeds](https://www.thespruceeats.com/what-is-nigella-sativa-1763087)
- [ ] 5 mangos *ripe but firm roughly chopped into 2 cm chunks*
- [ ] 350 ml (1 1/2 cups) white wine vinegar
- [ ] 400 g (2 cups) caster sugar (granulated sugar)

## Instructions

1. Add the ghee to a saucepan and heat over a medium heat.
2. Add the onion and fry, stirring often, for 6–8 minutes, until the onion starts to soften and turn translucent.
3. Add in the minced garlic, stir and cook for another minute.
4. Now add in the garam masala, Aseofoetida, salt, cumin seeds, and nigella seeds and fry, continuously stirring, for 2 minutes until the spices start to release their fragrance.
5. Add in the chopped mangos and stir together.
6. Now add in the sugar and white wine vinegar.
7. Stir everything together and bring to the boil.
8. Turn down the heat and gently simmer, uncovered, for one and a half hours, stirring a couple of times during cooking until you've got that signature chutney consistency.
9. Turn off the heat and allow to cool before serving.

## Notes

- This recipe makes approximately four 250ml jars of mango chutney (a little over 4 cups altogether).

#### **Storage**

You want to store the mango chutney in sterilized jars with a sealed lid. I use 250ml (8.5oz) jars. They can be kept in sealed jars in the refrigerator unopened for around 2–3 months. Once opened, so long as it’s kept covered and refrigerated in between uses, it should last around a month.

#### **Ingredients Swaps**

- Change this up by varying the amount of spices to suit your tastes. You can add a freshly chopped chilli or a teaspoon of minced ginger to give the mango chutney a kick of heat.
- Use a potato masher to mash up the large pieces of mango so you have a finer chutney.
- You can swap out the ghee for sunflower or olive oil if you haven’t got any in.
