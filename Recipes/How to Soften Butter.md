## Ingredients

- [ ] Butter

## Instructions

1. Setting out chilled butter at room temperature for 30 to 45 minutes prior to use gives it time to soften. The same approach works for frozen butter, though it can take a couple of hours.

   ![[soften-butter-on-countertop.png]]

2. Cutting butter into small cubes helps to soften the butter in about 15 minutes.
    1. Cut the stick of butter in half (the long way) and stack the halves on top of one another
    2. Cut the stacked halves in half again (the long way)
    3. Stack the sticks of butter and cut perpendicularly into small cubes

       ![[soften-butter-cut-into-chunks.png]]

3. Place the butter stick in a resealable plastic bag or between two sheets of waxed or parchment paper, then pound and roll with a rolling pin to flatten and soften.

   ![[soften-butter-with-rolling-pin.png]]

4. The quickest of the three methods is to grate the butter. Partially unwrap the butter and hold the wrapped portion while using the largest holes on a box grater to create a pile of freshly grated butter shavings that will soften in a matter of minutes.
5. Though it’s not recommended for baking, you can use the microwave to soften butter in a pinch. If you only want the butter to soften for spreading, microwave it on the defrost setting (30%) in 5-second increments until it’s softened as desired.

   ![[soften-butter-with-microwave.png.png]]
