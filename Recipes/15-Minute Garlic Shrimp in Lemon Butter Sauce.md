Garlic Shrimp in Lemon Butter Sauce – in less than 15 minutes! These little guys are cooked with a light lemon butter sauce that’s just enough to drizzle on steamed veggies or serve the shrimp on crusty bread! So easy and so delicious!

## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 tablespoon olive oil
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- [ ] 1 pound (ca. 454 g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
- [ ] 6–8 cloves of fresh garlic, minced
- [ ] 2 tablespoon lemon juice
- [ ] 1 tablespoon caper brine
- [ ] 1/4 cup chopped parsley
- [ ] 3 tablespoon butter, cut into cubes
- [ ] salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. **INFUSE:** Heat a large frying-pan over high heat. Add the olive oil and red pepper flakes and allow the spice to infuse into the oil for 30 seconds.
2. **SHRIMP:** Add the shrimp to the pan and spread it out so it cooks evenly. Cook for about 1 minute flipping as needed. Add the garlic, lemon juice, and caper brine to the frying-pan and let cook for 1 minute.
3. **SPRINKLE**: Add 1/2 of the parsley along with the cubes of butter and swirl the pan, so butter starts to melt slowly, about 2-3 minutes. As the butter melts it should start forming a thick type of sauce.
4. **FINISH:** Add the remaining parsley and stir to combine. If the sauce starts to thicken too much, you can remove the shrimp to a serving dish and add a teaspoon of water to help thin it out to a pourable consistency. Keep in mind that this recipe creates just enough sauce to coat the shrimp, but it is flavourful enough to serve with rice or pasta tossed in olive oil.

## Notes

- It's best to use fresh garlic for this recipe. I'm a big fan of minced garlic in a jar for most, but fresh does definitely taste better in this recipe!
- Caper brine is the liquid stuff capers come packed in.
