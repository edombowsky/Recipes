## Ingredients

- [ ] 2 pints (16 ounces) ripe cherry tomatoes, halved or quartered if large
- [ ] 1/4 cup soy sauce or tamari, plus more to taste
- [ ] 3 tablespoons rice vinegar, plus more to taste
- [ ] 2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil
- [ ] 1 cup (5 1/2 ounces) shelled, frozen, edamame (no need to defrost)
- [ ] 9 1/2 ounces dried soba noodles (see note)
- [ ] 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, soy sauce or tamari, rice vinegar and sesame oil. Stir well and let marinate while the edamame and noodles cook.
2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat. Add the edamame and then the soba and cook until the soba is just tender, but still has some bite, 4 to 5 minutes. Drain. Toss with the tomatoes until well coated.
3. Taste, and add more soy sauce and/or vinegar, if desired. Divide among four bowls and serve, with sesame seeds sprinkled on top.

## Notes

- This dish is even better after sitting in the fridge, so feel free to make it a day ahead.
- Soba is sold in packages of slightly different weights; this recipe works well with anything from 8 to 10 ounces of dried soba noodles.
- For a gluten-free dish, use tamari instead of soy sauce and look for 100-percent buckwheat soba.
- Can be made up to 1 day in advance and served cold.
- Refrigerate for up to 2 days.
- You may substitute the same weight of any tomatoes, chopped, for the cherry tomatoes.
