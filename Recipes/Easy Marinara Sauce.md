## Ingredients

- [ ] 3 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 1 cup onion finely diced
- [ ] ⅓ cup carrot shredded
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic minced
- [ ] 28 ounces (ca. 1,058 g) whole tomatoes canned
- [ ] 28 ounces (ca. 1,058 g) crushed tomatoes canned
- [ ] 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- [ ] 1-2 teaspoons sugar (optional)
- [ ] ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- [ ] ½ cup water
- [ ] ¼ cup fresh basil chopped
- [ ] ¼ teaspoon each salt and black pepper

## Instructions

1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. To the pot, add the finely diced onion, shredded carrot, and minced garlic. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
3. Add whole canned tomatoes (juice included), breaking them apart gently with a spoon.
4. Mix in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar if using, dried oregano, and season with ¼ teaspoon each salt and pepper. Stir in ½ cup of water.
5. Simmer uncovered on low heat for about 20 minutes, or until thickened to the desired consistency. Stir in fresh basil.
6. Serve over spaghetti or enjoy in your favourite recipes.

## Notes

- Fresh basil is recommended and should be added just before serving.
- Fresh basil can be substituted with 2 teaspoons of dried basil. If using dried, add it along with the oregano.
- Sugar may be required depending on the brand of tomatoes you purchase.
- Store leftover sauce in a covered container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Reheat on the stovetop until heated through.
