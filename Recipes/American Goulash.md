## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 pound (ca. 454 g) lean minced beef
- [ ] 1 large yellow onion chopped
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic minced
- [ ] 2 cups marinara sauce or tomato-based pasta sauce, approx. ½ of a 26 oz jar
- [ ] 2 cups beef broth or water (more as needed)
- [ ] 14 ounces (ca. 529 g) canned diced tomatoes undrained
- [ ] 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- [ ] 1 green bell pepper diced, optional
- [ ] 1 ½ teaspoons Italian seasoning
- [ ] 1 bay leaf 2 if they are small
- [ ] ½ teaspoon salt
- [ ] ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] 1 ½ cups elbow macaroni noodles uncooked, about 8 oz
- [ ] ½ cup cheddar or mozzarella cheese, shredded, optional

## Instructions

1. In a large frying-pan with a lid or a Dutch oven, cook the minced beef, onion, and garlic over medium-high heat until no pink remains. Drain any fat.
2. Add the marinara sauce, broth, diced tomatoes with juices, tomato paste, bell pepper (if using), Italian seasoning, & bay leaves. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
3. Add the elbow macaroni, reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
4. Uncover and simmer for an additional 5 to 10 minutes, or until the pasta is tender.
5. Remove & discard the bay leaf. Top with cheese if using, and replace the lid. Let rest for about 5 minutes or until the pasta has thickened and the cheese is melted.

## Notes

- This recipe can easily be doubled to feed a crowd (serving sizes can be adjusted in the print screen).
- For heartier appetites, the **meal can be stretched** with additional pasta (and water/broth), canned beans or chopped vegetables. (diced zucchini, sliced mushrooms, corn, or additional bell peppers). Any ground meat can be substituted for beef.
- Start with the amount of broth/water as listed and add extra if needed (depending on pasta shape). The goulash will thicken as it cools and rests. Depending on the size and shape of your pan, you may need to **add a little more liquid**. Keep an eye on the dish as it cooks, and add more liquid as required. The mixture will thicken slightly as it cools.
- Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days.
