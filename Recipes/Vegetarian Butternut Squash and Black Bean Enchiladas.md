## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 cup red enchilada sauce, [[Enchilada Sauce|homemade]] or canned
- [ ] 1 teaspoon olive oil
- [ ] 2 1/2 cups peeled butternut squash, cut 1/2-inch-dice
- [ ] salt and pepper, to taste
- [ ] 1 small onion, diced
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 jalapeño, seeded and diced
- [ ] 10 oz can Rotel tomatoes with green chillies
- [ ] 1 1/2 cups reduced sodium canned black beans, rinsed and drained
- [ ] 1/4 cup cilantro
- [ ] 1 teaspoons cumin
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoons chilli powder
- [ ] 1/4 cup water
- [ ] 8 medium low-carb whole wheat flour tortillas, I used La Tortilla Factory
- [ ] 1 cup reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese
- [ ] 2 tablespoons chopped spring onions, for garnish
- [ ] reduced-fat sour cream, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 400 °F (ca. 204 °C).
2. Place 1/4 cup enchilada sauce on the bottom of a large baking dish.
3. Heat olive oil over medium-high heat in large frying-pan. Add onions, garlic, and jalapeño and cook 2–3 minutes until onions become translucent and garlic is fragrant.
4. Add cubed butternut, Rotel tomatoes, black beans, water, cilantro, cumin, and chilli powder and season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the squash is tender, about 30 to 35 minutes.
5. Place about a generous 1/3 cup filling in the centre of each tortilla and roll, place on the baking dish seam side down. Repeat with the remaining filling.
6. Top with remaining enchilada sauce and cheese and bake, covered with foil until hot and the cheese is melted, about 10 minutes. Top with spring onions and eat with sour cream if desired.
