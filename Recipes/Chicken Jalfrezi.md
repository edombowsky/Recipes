## Ingredients

### Chicken

- [ ] 3 chicken breasts (about 500g/1.1lbs) chopped into bite-sized chunks
- [ ] 1 teaspoon cumin
- [ ] 1 teaspoon coriander
- [ ] 1 teaspoon turmeric
- [ ] 1 teaspoon garam masala

##### Sauce

- [ ] 1 tablespoon sunflower oil
- [ ] 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- [ ] 1 large onion peeled and sliced
- [ ] 1 red chilli sliced
- [ ] 1 green chilli sliced
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic peeled and minced
- [ ] 2 teaspoons minced ginger
- [ ] 1 teaspoon garam masala
- [ ] 2 teaspoons turmeric
- [ ] 1 ½ teaspoon salt
- [ ] 2 teaspoon cumin
- [ ] 2 teaspoon ground coriander
- [ ] 1 red bell pepper sliced
- [ ] 1 green bell pepper sliced
- [ ] 400 g (14 oz) tin chopped tomatoes
- [ ] 240 ml (1 cup) chicken stock ensure this is a gluten-free stock if required
- [ ] 1 tablespoon tomato purée paste for US

##### To Serve

- [ ] [[Pilau Rice]] or boiled rice
- [ ] fresh coriander
- [ ] chilli flakes
- [ ] sliced red onion

## Instructions

1. Coat the chicken in the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala and leave to marinade for 10 minutes, covered in the fridge.
2. Once the chicken has marinated, heat the oil and butter in a large frying pan and add the chicken. Cook on a high heat for 4-5 minutes until the chicken is sealed.
3. Add the onion to the pan, turn the heat down to low-medium and cook for 5 minutes, stirring often, until the onion softens.
4. Add the red and green chilli, garlic, and ginger and cook for a further 2 minutes.
5. Add in the garam masala, turmeric, salt, cumin, and ground coriander. Stir to coat the onions, and cook for a further 2 minutes.
6. Add in the red and green pepper, tinned tomatoes, chicken stock, and tomato purée.
7. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 10 minutes until thickened.
8. Stir in the fresh coriander and serve with rice, topped with fresh coriander, a few chilli flakes, and some sliced red onion.

## Notes

- **Can I make it ahead?** Yes, you can make the Jalfrezi ahead, then cool, cover and refrigerate for up to two days. Reheat in a pan over a medium heat until the chicken is hot throughout.
- **Will it freeze OK?** Yes, freeze in freezer-proof containers with a lid as soon as the curry is cool. Defrost overnight in the fridge, then reheat in a pan – as per above.
- **Can I make Chicken Jalfrezi in the slow cooker?** Yes – bring the curry up to the boil (step 12), then transfer to a slow cooker. Cook for 3–4 hours on high or 5–6 hours on low.
- **Swap out the chicken for lamb:** Lamb Jalfrezi is delicious – but it takes longer to cook to become tender. If you want to replace the chicken with lamb, then add a little more liquid (I would add an extra 140oz/400g tin of chopped tomatoes) and simmer for 2 hours, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of water or stock if it starts to look too dry.
