Frying-pan Lasagna Recipe – This quick and easy recipe is the perfect weeknight solution for the lasagna lover! Made in one pan, this frying-pan recipe is a definite favourite!

If you love lasagna like my family does – you are going to love this **quick and easy** Frying-pan Lasagna recipe! It’s a **great weeknight recipe** that’s so perfect for busy days. It’s hearty, delicious, and satisfying so pasta fans will love it – yet it is on the table in **30 minutes** and made all in **one pan**!

## Ingredients

- [ ] ▢ 1 tablespoon olive oil
- [ ] 1/2 medium onion, chopped
- [ ] 1 1/2 pounds minced beef
- [ ] 1 teaspoon [[Stone House Seasoning]]
- [ ] 1 1/2 teaspoons chopped fresh oregano (divided), or 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
- [ ] pinch red pepper flakes, optional
- [ ] 4 cups tomato sauce
- [ ] 8 lasagna noodles, broken into 2-inch pieces
- [ ] 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- [ ] 1/2 cup ricotta cheese, divided

## Instructions

1. Drizzle olive oil into a medium frying-pan set over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3 minutes. Add the minced beef and cook until browned throughout, about 5 more minutes. Discard any excess drippings.
2. Stir in the Stone House Seasoning, 1 teaspoon of the fresh oregano, red pepper flakes (if using), tomato sauce, and the broken noodle pieces. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to medium-low. Simmer, cover and cook until the noodles are tender, about 20 minutes.
3. Remove from the heat and top with Parmesan cheese, ricotta and remaining fresh oregano.

## Notes

- **To** **Make-Ahead**. Frying-pan Lasagna makes a great make-ahead meal. Simply cool completely, cover, and store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- **To store.** Additionally, you can store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- **Leftover Makeover**:

  **Meaty Cheesy Bread**

  Preheat the oven to 425ºF. Slice a loaf of French bread in half lengthwise. Brush the bread with 1 tablespoon olive oil mixed with 1/2 teaspoon Stone House Seasoning. Toast the bread until golden brown, about 10 minutes. Ladle on leftover lasagna and top with 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese. Bake until the cheese has melted, and the lasagna is warm throughout.
