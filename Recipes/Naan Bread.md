## Ingredients

- [ ] ½ cup warm water not hot, 120 mL
- [ ] 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast 1 ¼-oz package, 7 g
- [ ] 1 teaspoon sugar
- [ ] 3 cups all-purpose flour 360 g
- [ ] 1 teaspoon salt
- [ ] ¾ cup full-fat yogurt 180 g

## Instructions

1. **Proof yeast**: Combine warm water with yeast and sugar. After 5 to 10 minutes, the mixture should be frothy.
2. **Combine with flour**: In a large bowl (or the bowl of your Kitchen Aid), combine flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and yogurt.
3. **Knead**: Knead by hand (8 minutes) or with the bread hook of a standing mixer (5 minutes at medium speed), until dough is smooth. The dough will be sticky, but if it is too sticky to handle, add flour 1 tablespoon at a time.
4. **Rise**: Place dough in a lightly greased bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Set in a warm place* and let rise for 1 to 2 hours, or until dough has doubled in size.
5. **Divide**: On a well-floured surface, flatten dough into a disk, then cut that disk into 8 pieces.
6. **Cook**: Heat a cast iron frying-pan or heavy-bottomed pan over medium/high heat. Working one at a time, roll each piece of naan on a floured surface to about 1/4 inch thick (it's fine if the shape isn't perfect). Cook in the frying-pan, about 2 minutes per side (until golden brown with slightly charred spots). Cover with a towel to keep warm while you finish the rest.

## Notes

- **To make cheese naan** cut the dough into 16 pieces (rather than 8). Roll each out to ⅛-inch. Sprinkle a few tablespoons of mozzarella cheese into the centre, top with another piece, and crimp the edges to seal. Cook as instructed above.
- **Store** **naan** in an airtight container at room temp for a few days. To reheat, wrap in a damp paper towel and microwave for 15 seconds.
