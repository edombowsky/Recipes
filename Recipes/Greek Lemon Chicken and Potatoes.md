## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 cup chicken stock
- [ ] 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
- [ ] 1/2 cup olive oil
- [ ] 1 shallot, diced
- [ ] 1 tablespoon dried oregano
- [ ] 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
- [ ] 3 pounds (ca. 1,361 g) Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges (about 8 medium potatoes)
- [ ] 4 boneless, skin-on chicken breasts (2 to 3 pounds (ca. 1,361 g))

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 450 °F (ca. 232 °C).
2. In a broiler-safe 9×13-inch casserole dish, combine the chicken stock, lemon juice, olive oil, shallot, oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk to combine, then add the potatoes, tossing to thoroughly coat.
3. Roast the potatoes, stirring halfway through, until they are becoming tender but still have light resistance when poked with a fork, about 40 minutes.
4. Place the chicken breasts skin-side up on top of the par-cooked potatoes and baste with some juices from the pan. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then roast until the chicken reaches 165 °F (ca. 74 °C) in the thickest parts and the skin is crisp and golden brown, 20 to 40 minutes, depending on the size of your chicken breasts.
5. Let the chicken rest for at least 5 minutes.
6. Broil the potatoes (optional): Set chicken aside on a cutting board and tent with foil. Preheat the broiler to high. Set the pan of potatoes 3 to 4 inches under the broiler until most of the potatoes are golden brown, 2 to 4 minutes.
7. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and consume within 3 days. Leftovers are best reheated at 350 °F (ca. 177 °C) for about 15 minutes.
