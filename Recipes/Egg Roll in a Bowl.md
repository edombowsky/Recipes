## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 lb (ca. 454 g) ground pork
- [ ] 1 small head cabbage, thinly sliced
- [ ] 1 large carrot, julienne
- [ ] 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
- [ ] 1/4 cup soy sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- [ ] 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- [ ] 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce (optional)
- [ ] Salt and black pepper to taste
- [ ] Green onions and sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions

1. Heat a large frying-pan over medium-high heat and add the ground pork. Cook until browned and crumbled, then remove excess fat.
2. Add the sliced cabbage, carrot, and onion to the frying-pan with the pork. Cook, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
3. Add the garlic and ginger to the frying-pan, sauté for another minute until fragrant.
4. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sriracha sauce. Pour over the pork and vegetable mixture in the frying-pan.
5. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Cook for an additional 2–3 minutes to let the flavours meld together.
6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
7. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds before serving.

## Notes

Wait until the entire dish is complete and then remove from Heat and add the cabbage, fold it in and cover and let it sit for 5 minutes. That will soften the Cabbage enough and leave a little crunch with it. And if you add the cabbage in the frying-pan when you're cooking the carrots and onions you will overcook the cabbage.
