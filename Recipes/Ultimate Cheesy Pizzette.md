Sometimes you just want to impress a room full of people without actually trying. Enter: **Pizzette**. These _bite-sized_, _cheesy_, _crowd-pleasing_ little snacks are my go-to party trick when I need something _quick_ but still want everyone to think I spent hours in the kitchen.

These tiny pizzas are here to show up every party snack you’ve ever made—crispy, saucy, cheesy, and done in 20 minutes flat. Made with fluffy dinner rolls, rich tomato sauce, gooey mozzarella, and fresh rosemary, they’re basically pizza’s cute, irresistible cousin.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 20 dinner rolls
- [ ] 3 cups ready-made tomato sauce
- [ ] 11 ounces (0.42 kg) buffalo mozzarella
- [ ] 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
- [ ] Extra-virgin olive oil
- [ ] Salt
- [ ] Black pepper

## Instructions

1. **Preheat the Oven**: Preheat your oven to 350 °F (ca. 177 °C) to get it ready for pizzette magic.
2. **Prep the Rolls**: Slice each dinner roll in half and place them cut-side up on a baking sheet.
3. **Add the Sauce**: Spoon a layer of tomato sauce over each roll half, just enough to cover the surface.
4. **Top with Mozzarella**: Cut the mozzarella into small cubes. Add about 2 teaspoons of cheese to the centre of each pizzetta.
5. **Season and Bake**: Sprinkle with rosemary, a pinch of salt, and a bit of black pepper. Bake for about 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and slightly bubbly. Keep an eye on them—some ovens run hot.
6. **Finish and Serve**: Pull them out, drizzle each one with a little olive oil, and serve immediately while hot and delicious.

## Notes

🧀 **Mozzarella Matters (But Let’s Not Get Snobby)**  
Buffalo mozzarella is dreamy, yes—but if all you’ve got is regular shredded mozzarella from a bag, guess what? It still melts. It still tastes like cheese. You’re good.

🍞 **Bread Rolls, Not a Science Project**  
Any soft dinner rolls work—leftover burger buns? Yup. That weird bag of Hawaiian rolls you forgot about? Even better. Just avoid anything too crusty or dense unless you want pizza croutons.

🍅 **Sauce: Store-Bought is Fine™**  
Look, we all _want_ to simmer tomato sauce for six hours like an Italian nonna, but this is a 20-minute snack, not a therapy session. Jarred marinara is your best friend—bonus points if it’s garlic-heavy.

🌿 **Rosemary, Thyme, Whatever You’ve Got**  
Fresh rosemary is fancy and fragrant, but if you’ve only got dried Italian seasoning from 2007, go ahead and live your truth. Just don’t overdo it—this isn’t a forest.

🔥 **Bake, Don’t Broil (Unless You Like Drama)**  
Stick to baking at 350°F. Broiling sounds faster until you realize you’ve accidentally cremated your pizzette while checking your texts.

💡 **Make It Your Own (aka Fridge-Cleanout Night)**  
Toss on olives, pepperoni, mushrooms, or that one sad slice of prosciutto you’ve been ignoring. These little guys are ridiculously forgiving and way more flexible than you’d think.

🧊 **Leftovers? Ha. Cute.**  
You _won’t_ have any. But if you do, reheat them in the oven—not the microwave—unless you like your pizzette soggy and sad.
