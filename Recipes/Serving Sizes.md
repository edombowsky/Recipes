## Rice

### Brown Basmati

For brown basmati rice, the standard serving size is generally considered to be:
- Cooked: 1/2 cup (approximately 100 grams or 3.5 ounces)
- Uncooked: 1/4 cup (approximately 45 grams or 1.6 ounces)
- This cooked portion contains about 150-160 calories, making it a suitable amount for a single meal portion, especially when combined with other components like vegetables and proteins.

[[How Much is a Serving of Brown Basmati Rice Explained]]

### Jasmine

A typical serving size of jasmine rice is about 1/2 cup (cooked). This amount translates to approximately 75 to 80 grams, depending on how the rice is cooked. In terms of uncooked rice, a serving size is usually about 1/4 cup, which is around 45 to 50 grams, and it expands during cooking to yield the 1/2 cup serving. It’s important to note that serving sizes can vary based on dietary needs, the specific meal, and individual appetite.

### Carrots

A standard serving of carrots is approximately **61 grams** (about **1 medium carrot**) or **1/2 cup** of chopped carrots.  For baby carrots, a serving is typically **8 to 10 pieces** (approximately **80–100 grams** or **3 ounces**). 

- **Medium Carrot**: ~61g, containing about 25 calories. 
- **Chopped Carrots**: 1/2 cup (~64g) or 1 cup (~128g). 
- **Baby Carrots**: 8–10 pieces (~3oz/85g), containing about 30 calories. 
- **Raw Whole Carrot (FDA)**: A 7-inch long carrot with a 1-1/4 inch diameter is ~78g.
