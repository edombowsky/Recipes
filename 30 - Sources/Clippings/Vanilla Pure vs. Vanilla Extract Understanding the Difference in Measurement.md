## What is Pure Vanilla?

Pure vanilla, also known as vanilla absolute or vanilla essence, is a concentrated oil extracted from vanilla beans. It is a thick, dark brown liquid with a rich, creamy flavour and aroma. Pure vanilla is often used in high-end baking and cooking, as it provides a deep, intense flavour that is unmatched by vanilla extract.

### How is Pure Vanilla Made?

Pure vanilla is made by extracting the oil from vanilla beans using a solvent, such as ethanol or propylene glycol. The extraction process involves several steps, including:

- Harvesting: Vanilla beans are harvested from the vanilla orchid, a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.
- Curing: The beans are cured in a process that involves sweating, drying, and curing to develop the flavour and aroma.
- Extraction: The cured beans are then extracted using a solvent to produce a concentrated oil.

## What is Vanilla Extract?

Vanilla extract, on the other hand, is a diluted form of pure vanilla. It is made by mixing pure vanilla with a solvent, such as ethanol or glycerin, and water. The resulting liquid is a lighter colour and has a milder flavour than pure vanilla.

### How is Vanilla Extract Made?

Vanilla extract is made by mixing pure vanilla with a solvent and water. The mixture is then filtered and bottled. The resulting liquid is a standardized extract that contains a specific amount of vanillin, the active compound responsible for the flavour and aroma of vanilla.

## Key Differences Between Pure Vanilla and Vanilla Extract

There are several key differences between pure vanilla and vanilla extract:

- **Concentration**: Pure vanilla is a concentrated oil, while vanilla extract is a diluted form of pure vanilla.
- **Flavor**: Pure vanilla has a rich, intense flavour, while vanilla extract has a milder flavour.
- **Aroma**: Pure vanilla has a strong, creamy aroma, while vanilla extract has a lighter aroma.
- **Usage**: Pure vanilla is often used in small amounts, while vanilla extract is used in larger quantities.

### Why You Can’t Use the Same Amount of Pure Vanilla as Vanilla Extract

Using the same amount of pure vanilla as vanilla extract can lead to disappointing results. Pure vanilla is a concentrated oil, and using too much of it can overpower the other flavours in your recipe. On the other hand, using too little vanilla extract may not provide enough flavour.

## How to Use Pure Vanilla and Vanilla Extract Correctly

To use pure vanilla and vanilla extract correctly, follow these guidelines:

- **Pure Vanilla**: Use 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon of pure vanilla per recipe. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste.
- **Vanilla Extract**: Use 1 to 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract per recipe. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste.

### Converting Pure Vanilla to Vanilla Extract

If a recipe calls for pure vanilla, but you only have vanilla extract, you can convert the amount using the following ratio:

- 1/4 teaspoon pure vanilla = 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla = 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
